# Reconstructed voice guide

The original voice guide was not present in repository history. This file reconstructs only the rules supplied for v6; it is not an exact recovered original.

## Target voice

- A practitioner explaining mechanics to a peer.
- Direct, specific, human, and comfortable with short paragraphs.
- Declarative narrative openings; mechanism before consequence.
- Product judgment grounded in a real decision, trade-off, failure mode, measurement, or artefact.
- Indian English spelling where it occurs naturally, without forced analogies.
- Quiet confidence. No hype, corporate thought-leadership clichés, or obvious AI symmetry.

## Strong default shape

Use `Incident → Mechanism → Decision → Artifact` when the evidence supports it. Do not invent an incident or mechanically force all four stages.

- Reach: incident or observation → mechanism → implication.
- Authority: incident or problem → mechanism → decision.
- Opportunity: problem → decision → artefact → evidence.
- Humour: observation → mechanism → quiet punchline.
- Framework: real problem → framework → practical decision.

## Open and close

Open with a specific scene, visible failure, calculation, named mechanism, or consequential observation. Reject generic listicle and trend openings.

Close with an earned declarative line or a specific invited question that asks for a concrete experience. Never beg for engagement.

## Banned language

`delve`, `leverage` as a verb, `tapestry`, `game-changer`, `revolutionary`, `unlock`, `unleash`, `in today's fast-paced world`, `let's dive in`, `navigate the complexities`, `furthermore`, `moreover`, `agree or disagree`, `what do you think`, `drop your thoughts below`, emoji stacks, generic numbered principle lists, and TED-talk closers.

## Honesty

Never invent a personal incident, statistic, quotation, ownership claim, result, customer, or credential. Source traceability does not replace human verification.
