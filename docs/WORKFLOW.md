# LinkedIn Authority OS v6

## North Star
Qualified speaking and podcast invitations. Secondary outcomes: advisory/project inbound and relevant senior product opportunities.

## Content thesis
The strongest structure for Abhillash is:

`Incident → Mechanism → Decision → Artifact`

Each post must do at least three of these four jobs. Generic news summaries are rejected.

## Pipeline

### 1. Goal Router
Classify the requested post as:
- `reach`: incident, humour, cultural observation;
- `authority`: mechanism, architecture, framework, trade-off;
- `opportunity`: proof, artifact, case study, decision record.

### 2. Scout
Collect current GenAI product signal from primary sources first: company research, papers, engineering blogs, GitHub repositories, official announcements. Secondary sources are discovery inputs only.

### 3. Analyst
Cluster the signal, deep-read the best evidence, deduplicate against prior posts, and propose a differentiated thesis. The output must identify what happened, what mechanism caused it, what decision a builder should make, and what evidence or artifact supports the claim.

### 4. Writer
Create three candidates in Abhillash's voice. The writer must read the voice anchors before drafting. It must not write generic LinkedIn prose, engagement bait, or inflated claims.

### 5. Critic
Use independent gates for hook, escalation, evidence, judgment, voice, opportunity conversion, and format fit. Claims without traceable evidence fail. A draft that merely sounds polished does not pass.

### 6. Human Approval
Never auto-publish. Show the winning draft, sources, critic notes, and suggested format. Abhillash approves or edits.

### 7. Learning Loop
At 24 and 72 hours record impressions, reach, external comments, saves, sends, reposts, profile visits, relevant follows, artifact clicks, and inbound opportunities. Separate organic and paid performance.

## Weekly rhythm
- Monday: incident-led reach post
- Tuesday: mechanism/framework authority post
- Thursday: proof/artifact post
- Friday: professional viewpoint or short demo
- Weekend: analytics review and optional long-form article

Default: 3–5 strong posts weekly. Do not force seven posts.

## Hard rules
- Primary evidence for factual claims.
- No invented incidents, quotes, numbers, or credentials.
- Each post is standalone; no dependency on a series.
- No generic 'what do you think?' closer.
- Do not optimise only for impressions or reactions.
- Preserve Abhillash's natural rhythm and judgment.
